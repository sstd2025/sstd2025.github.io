Attractions/Events in Osaka

Expo 2025

A World Expo is an event that brings together people and innovations from around the world in an effort to address issues facing humankind on a global scale. The Osaka Expo 1970, the first ever held in Japan and Asia, became a symbol of Japan's rapid economic growth and one of the country's most lauded events. Twenty years after the Aichi Expo 2005, Expo 2025 Osaka, Kansai will bring the international community back to Japan.

Schedule: 184 days from April 13 to October 13, 2025 (Including during SSTD 2025: August 25-27)
Expo2025 Venue: Yumeshima, Osaka (about 30 min. by Osaka Metro from the SSTD 2025 venue)
Webpage: https://www.expo2025.or.jp/en/

Osaka Castle Park

Osaka Castle is one of Japan's most famous landmarks and it played a major role in the unification of Japan during the sixteenth century. The castle museum documents the history of the castle and the city and features artifacts of the castle dwellers, including armour, weapons, and documents. The castle tower offers panoramic views of the city, and the surrounding park is famous for its cherry blossoms in spring.

Hours: 9:00-17:00 (Last admission 16:30)
Access: 10-minute walk from Tanimachi 4-chome Station (Subway)
Website: https://www.osakacastle.net/english/

Dotonbori District

Dotonbori is Osaka's most famous tourist destination and a food lover's paradise. This vibrant district is known for its dazzling neon signs, enormous 3D signage (including the famous Glico Running Man), and countless restaurants serving Osaka's best local cuisine. Don't miss trying local specialties like takoyaki (octopus balls), okonomiyaki (savory pancakes), and kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers).

Best Time to Visit: Evening for the full atmosphere and lighting
Access: Namba Station or Nipponbashi Station
Must-Try Foods: Takoyaki, Okonomiyaki, Kushikatsu, Ramen

Nakanoshima District

The SSTD 2025 venue, Osaka University Nakanoshima Center, is located in Nakanoshima, a vibrant district in the heart of Osaka. This island, nestled between the Dojima and Tosabori rivers, offers a unique blend of culture, history, and nature, all within easy walking distance of the conference.

City Central Public Hall

(5-min walk)

Museum of Art, Osaka
Modern and contemporary art in a striking building. (8-min walk)

Osaka Science Museum
Interactive exhibits and a planetarium. (10-min walk)

Osaka Prefectural Nakanoshima Library
Historic library with impressive architecture. (7-min walk)

Day Trips to Kyoto & Nara

Both Kyoto and Nara are easily accessible from Osaka for day trips:

Kyoto (30-45 mins by train)
- Famous temples and shrines including Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)
- Historic Gion district with traditional architecture and geishas
- Arashiyama bamboo forest and monkey park
- Fushimi Inari Shrine with thousands of torii gates

Nara (45 mins by train)
- Todaiji Temple housing Japan's largest Buddha statue
- Nara Park with friendly deer roaming freely
- Historic temples and shrines in a peaceful setting
- Beautiful traditional gardens
